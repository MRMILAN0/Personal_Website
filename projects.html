Master Thesis:

Thermal Analysis of Electromagnetic Docking and Undocking System for 3U CubeSats in Low Earth Orbit

Julius-Maximilians-Universität Würzburg, Germany | 2024

This Master Thesis focused on evaluating and optimizing the thermal performance of the TAMARIW CubeSat docking module to ensure reliable operation in space. Key outcomes include:

  • Thermal Simulation

    Conducted under sun-synchronous orbital conditions to evaluate temperature profiles.

  • System Design

    Redesigned docking panel cover with heat sinks and selected high-performance TIM.

  • Component Testing

    Verified Raspberry Pi camera operability at extreme cold temperatures.

  • Subsystem Analysis

    Identified thermal issues and ensured compliance with operational ranges.

  • Recommendations

    Suggested additional subsystems for future simulations and thermal vacuum tests.

This work provides valuable insights into designing effective thermal control systems for CubeSat missions.
